Swedish Meatballs

Description

A Midwest classic dish, Swedish Meatballs are a welcome addition to any family dinner or potluck! Served in gravy and perfect for putting on mashed potatoes or buttered noodles, these meatballs are sure to satisfy the meat-lover in your life!

Ingredients

Meatballs:

  • 2lbs ground beef (a mix of 1-85% and 1-93%)
  • 1 c. Pankko bread crumbs
  • 1-1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1/8 tsp. allspice (or even less-just a pinch)
  • 1 tsp. corn starch
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 2/3 c. whipping cream

Gravy:

  • 2-3T. butter
  • 3 tsp. flour
  • 2 beef bouillon cubes dissolved in 2 c. hot water
  • Dash pepper
  • 1-1/2 tsp. sugar
  • 1/2 tsp. salt

Steps

Meatballs:

  1. In a small bowl, combine breadcrumbs, spices, and cornstarch.
  2. Mix the dry ingredients with the ground beef mixture.
  3. Blend together the beaten egg and whipping cream and thoroughly incorporate into the meatball mixture.
  4. Shape into 24-26 balls.
  5. Heat a drizzle (2-3 T) of olive oil in a large fry pan or electric skillet.
  6. Fry the meatballs in the hot oil, browning well on all sides.
  7. Transfer meatballs to a casserole or baking dish.

Gravy:

  1. In the same pan or skillet, mix flour and 2-3 T butter to make a roux.
  2. Dissolve the beef bouillon in water.
  3. Slowly add the water/bouillon mixture to the roux, heating until bubbly, smooth, and slightly thickened.
  4. Add spices to the gravy.
  5. Pour the gravy over the meatballs.
  6. Bake the meatballs and gravy uncovered at 350 degrees for 30 minutes.
  7. Remove from oven and serve!
